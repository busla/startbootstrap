What is FIS?

FIS is a web application that analyzes the log files from TK Strike, a Taekwondo scoring system, made by Daedo. FIS reads the logs as they are generated during the fight and creates statistics from that data. Competitors can access their profile in our database and get important statistics about their performance, as well as suggestions on what they need to improve.

Our web service streams the data LIVE so the audience can view the important details from the fights, while they are happening. The audience that didn´t make it to tournament, well, they can watch it as news feed on their mobile phone.

We´ve also designed a new gamestyle that we call Survival. Remember Tekken or Mortal Combat? Yes, we also have a lifebar. The competitor gets a total life in the beginning. Let´s say 250 kN. Daedo PSS measures the impact made to the chest guards and we deduct that impact from the opponent. The deduction for punches and headkicks is configured by the tournament manager. How does it work?

From kick to greater results

  • 1.

    A kick is made

    Every time the contestant scores by kicking in the opponents’ PSS, the score is written in a log file, among with information on it; how heavy it was, at what time it was made and more. FIS-Score instantly reads the log file and publishes it live on the website.

  • 2.

    Statistics are created...

    FIS-Score then uses the logs to create timeline of the fight, as well as show the results and the statistics from the fight, which can be used to further analyze the fight.

  • 3.

    ...and added to the user profile

    FIS-Score and FIS-members are then linked together, automatically adding the score from the fight to the contestant’s user profile in FIS-Members.

  • 4.

    Used in the next contest

    The contestants and her coach can then use that information to focus on skills that need improvement.

  • 5.

    Leading to better results

    Precise calculations and information on the fight, used to improve coaching, will in the end lead to improved result for the contestant.

FIS-Score

Live updates in your phone!

Score is an informational app and a website that analyses the Daedo PSS logs and publishes them in a comprehensible manner for the general viewer.

While the fight is still going on, FIS-Score publishes when a touch was made and how heavy it was, as a news feed. Between rounds and after the match it publishes further information, such as how many kicks were made and how many actually counted as scores, number of head kicks and referee points.

FIS-Score is a tool that makes Taekwondo competitions more comprehensible for the general viewer. In addition to that, it can be used by federations and tournament organizers in order to make Taekwondo competitions more transparant. For coaches, it is useful to analyze their trainees' fights and use the statistics to improve their results. In general, it adds statistical information to the sport, adding to its development and increasing results!

FIS-Survival

A whole new game!

FIS-Survival

The gamestyle everyone has been waiting for. Competitors get equal amount of "life" in kN and for each kick or punch, their total life get´s deducted. For each kick to the hogu, the competitor looses that same amount of life.

Users can set the total life in kN for competitors and how much life get´s deducted for punches and headkicks. Are you tired of playing Tekken or Mortal Combat? Find a club and start training. FIS is right around the corner.

Audience

For better understanding

Live updates with FIS-Score are specially designed for the audience on Taekwondo tournaments. With information screens or with the app and website on your phone or tablet you can get visual information on the fight, while it is happening. For example, how heavy a kick was, at what time it was made, whether there were pension points and more. The audience will also be able to use FIS-Members to access information on contestants.

Practitioners

For everyone who trains Taekwondo

FIS-Members and FIS-Score are not exclusively for contestants on big tournaments. It is also a useful tool for the gneral Taekwondo Practicioner. In clubs which decide to use FIS to keep track of their members, each practicioner will get a unique user profile. They can use the system to access drills and exercises from their coach.

Tournaments

Convenient solution

Set up a simple program on the score computers, plug in the already set-up server and you are good to go. The information from the scoring system will be published on a special website, making the audience able to access it on their phone, tablets or computers. It is also possible to publish the information on an info screen at the tournament.

You can use FIS-Members to register contestants on the tournament, making it possible for the result to be automatically added to their user profile.
